About MJ

Michael Jordan, often considered the greatest basketball player of all time, was born on February 17, 1963, in Brooklyn, New York. He played 15 seasons in the NBA, primarily with the Chicago Bulls, leading them to six championships and earning six Finals MVP awards. Known for his scoring ability, defensive skills, and unmatched competitiveness, Jordan became a global icon and helped popularize basketball worldwide. Beyond the court, he has succeeded as a businessman and owner of the Charlotte Hornets. His legacy continues to inspire athletes and fans across generations.
